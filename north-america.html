Red Wolf

  • Scientific Name: Canis rufus
  • Habitat: Historically found throughout the southeastern United States, now confined to a small area in North Carolina.
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered
  • Characteristics: The Red Wolf is smaller than the Grey Wolf but larger than the coyote. It has a reddish coat and is known for its elusive nature. Red wolves are social animals that live in packs and primarily hunt small mammals, deer, and other game. They are critically endangered due to habitat loss, hybridization with coyotes, and past persecution.
  • Biggest Threat: Captivity, Habitat Fragmentation, Climate Change

    American Bison

  • Scientific Name: Bison bison bison
  • Habitat: Historically widespread across the Great Plains of North America, now found in protected areas and private lands.
  • Conservation Status: Near Threatened
  • Characteristics: The American Bison is the largest land mammal in North America. It has a shaggy, dark brown coat and a distinctive hump on its shoulders. Bison live in herds and graze on grasses. Although once near extinction due to overhunting and habitat loss, conservation efforts have helped their populations recover, but they remain vulnerable due to limited genetic diversity and habitat fragmentation.
  • Biggest Threat: Habitat Loss

    Canada Lynx

  • Scientific Name: Lynx canadensis
  • Habitat: Boreal forests across Canada, Alaska, and parts of the northern United States.
  • Conservation Status: Threatened
  • Characteristics: The Canada Lynx is a medium-sized cat with tufted ears, large paws adapted for walking on snow, and a short tail. They primarily hunt snowshoe hares and are well-adapted to cold, snowy environments. The main threats to their survival include habitat loss due to logging, climate change, and competition with other predators.
  • Biggest Threat: Small population size, habitat loss and fragmentation from large wildfires, and climate change

    Black Footed Ferret

  • Scientific Name: Mustela nigripes
  • Habitat: Native to the Great Plains of North America, particularly in areas with prairie dog colonies.
  • Conservation Status: Endangered
  • Characteristics: Black-footed ferrets are slender, with black markings on their feet, tail tip, and face. They are nocturnal and depend heavily on prairie dogs for both food and shelter. Their populations declined drastically due to habitat loss and disease, but conservation efforts, including captive breeding and reintroduction programs, have been implemented to help their recovery.
  • Biggest Threat: Habitat loss and non-native disease
