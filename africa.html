Addax

  • Scientific Name: Addax nasomaculatus
  • Common Names: White antelope, Screwhorn antelope
  • Habitat: Native to the Sahara Desert in Africa.
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered
  • Characteristics: The addax is known for its long, twisted horns and sandy-colored coat that helps it blend into its desert surroundings. They are well-adapted to arid environments and can go long periods without water, obtaining moisture from the plants they eat.
  • Biggest Threat: Habitat encroachment

    African Bush Elephant

  • Scientific Name: Loxodonta africana
  • Common Names: African savanna elephant
  • Habitat: Various habitats across sub-Saharan Africa, including savannas, forests, deserts, and marshes.
  • Conservation Status: Vulnerable
  • Characteristics: The African bush elephant is the largest land animal on Earth. They have large ears shaped like the African continent, which help them regulate body temperature. They are herbivorous, feeding on grasses, fruits, and bark. They play a crucial role in their ecosystem by shaping the landscape and dispersing seeds.
  • Biggest Threat: Poaching for tusks, habitat loss and fragmentation

    Black Rhino

  • Scientific Name: Diceros bicornis
  • Common Names: Hook-lipped rhinoceros
  • Habitat: Various habitats in eastern and southern Africa, including savannas, grasslands, and forests.
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered
  • Characteristics: Black rhinos have two horns made of keratin. They are browsers, primarily feeding on bushes and trees. Despite their name, their color varies from brown to grey. They are known for their aggressive behavior and solitary nature.
  • Biggest Threat: Illegal Poaching, Competing species, Habitat changes

    Mountain Gorilla

  • Scientific Name: Gorilla beringei beringei
  • Habitat: Mountainous regions of central Africa, specifically in the Virunga volcanic mountains of Rwanda, Uganda, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo.
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered
  • Characteristics: Mountain gorillas are a subspecies of the eastern gorilla. They have thicker fur compared to other gorilla species, which helps them survive in cold mountain climates. They are primarily herbivorous, feeding on leaves, stems, and bamboo shoots. They live in social groups led by a dominant silverback male.
    • Biggest Threat: Poaching (for zoos), habitat loss
