Iberian lynx

The Iberian lynx, native to the Iberian Peninsula in Spain and Portugal, is one of the world's most endangered feline species. Habitat loss, decline in prey populations, and vehicle collisions have contributed to their critically low numbers. Conservation efforts, including breeding programs and habitat restoration, are in place to help their recovery.

European mink

The European mink is a small, semi-aquatic mammal found in Eastern Europe. It is critically endangered due to habitat destruction, pollution, and competition with the invasive American mink. Conservation projects are focusing on habitat protection and controlling the population of American minks to save this species.

Mediterranean Monk Seal

The Mediterranean monk seal is one of the world's rarest marine mammals, found in the Mediterranean Sea and the eastern Atlantic Ocean. Human activities, such as fishing and coastal development, have drastically reduced their population. Conservation actions include protecting their habitats and reducing human disturbances.

Saiga Antelope

The Saiga antelope, with its distinctive bulbous nose, once roamed across the Eurasian steppes. It is now critically endangered due to poaching and habitat loss. Efforts to protect the Saiga include anti-poaching measures and establishing protected areas in their natural habitats.
