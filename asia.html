Orangutan

Orangutans, native to Borneo and Sumatra, are critically endangered due to habitat loss from logging and palm oil plantations, illegal wildlife trade, and slow reproduction rates. Efforts to protect them include habitat preservation, rescue and rehabilitation, and combating illegal trade. Conservation measures are crucial as these intelligent great apes face ongoing threats that endanger their survival.

Bawean Deer

The Bawean deer, found on Bawean Island in Indonesia, is critically endangered due to habitat destruction and limited range. Conservation efforts focus on habitat protection and population monitoring to ensure the survival of this rare species.

Rhino

Rhinos, native to Africa and Asia, face critical endangerment due to poaching for their horns and habitat loss. Conservation efforts include anti-poaching measures, habitat restoration, and breeding programs to prevent extinction.

×

Pangolin

Pangolins, native to Asia and Africa, are critically endangered due to illegal wildlife trade and habitat loss. Efforts to protect them include strengthening anti-poaching laws, habitat preservation, and rehabilitation programs for rescued animals.
